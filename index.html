Roast Venison

List of igridents

  • 1 whole venison leg or roast
  • 4 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 2 tbsp juniper berries (crushed)
  • 1 tbsp dried thyme
  • 1 tbsp dried rosemary
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or melted butter
  • 1 cup strong dark ale or mead
  • 2 large onions, quartered
  • Fresh herbs for garnish

How to make it

  1. Preheat your hearth (oven) to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Rub the venison leg with salt, pepper, and the crushed garlic.
  3. Mix the crushed juniper berries, thyme, and rosemary.
  4. Coat the meat with olive oil or melted butter, then rub the herb mix all over.
  5. Place the onions in the bottom of your roasting pan, and lay the venison on top.
  6. Pour the dark ale or mead over the venison.
  7. Roast in the oven for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare.
  8. Let the meat rest for 15 minutes before slicing. Even Odin knows the value of patience — and letting the juices settle.

Fire-Roasted Hen Of The North

List of igridents

  • 1 whole hen (or free-range chicken, about 1.5–2 kg)
  • 5 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 2 tbsp wild honey
  • 2 tbsp mustard seeds, ground
  • 1 tbsp dried thyme
  • 1 tbsp dried sage
  • A handful of dried lingonberries or cranberries (optional)
  • Coarse salt and cracked black pepper
  • 3 tbsp animal fat or butter
  • 1 cup of mead (or dark ale)
  • 1 large onions, quartered
  • 2 parsnips, cut thick
  • A few sprigs of fresh rosemary or pine needles for garnish

How to make it

  1. Preheat your hearth (oven) to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Rinse the bird well, pat it dry, and rub her with salt, pepper, and the crushed garlic.
  3. In a horn or bowl, stir together honey, mustard seed, sage, and thyme. Melt the animal fat and combine with the mix.
  4. Brush the hen all over with the spiced fat mixture. Stuff her belly with the onion, parsnips, and dried berries if using.
  5. Place the onions in the bottom of your roasting pan, and lay the venison on top.
  6. Pour the dark ale or mead over the bird.
  7. Roast in the oven for about 1:15 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) for
  8. Rest the hen for 15 minutes, covered with cloth.
  9. Adorn with rosemary or fresh-picked pine needles. Even the shieldmaidens of old knew — the juicier the hen, the louder the feast

Frostfire Mead Cocktail

List of igridents

  • 1 cup mead (honey wine)
  • ½ shot spiced rum (or whiskey, for a stronger kick)
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 slice of fresh ginger
  • A small pinch of dried juniper berries (optional)
  • Orange peel (for garnish)

How to make it

  1. In a small pot, combine the mead, rum, honey, cloves, cinnamon stick, ginger, and juniper berries.
  2. Heat gently over low heat (do not boil), stirring until the honey dissolves and the mixture is aromatic — about 5–7 minutes.
  3. Strain the drink into a rustic mug or chalice.
  4. Garnish with a twist of orange peel and a sprinkle of cinnamon if desired.
  5. Serve warm — and toast to Odin under the winter moon!
