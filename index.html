Roast Veal

List of igridents

  • 1 whole venison leg or roast (about 3-4 lbs) — fit for a god or a mighty warrior!
  • 4 cloves of garlic, crushed — because even Odin needs to keep those pesky frost giants away
  • 2 tbsp juniper berries (crushed) — nature’s Norse magic spice
  • 1 tbsp dried thyme — the herb of wisdom (like Odin’s!)
  • 1 tbsp dried rosemary — as sharp as Odin’s spear, Gungnir
  • Salt and black pepper — for the bite of a raven’s beak
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or melted butter — to keep the meat as smooth as Mimir’s well
  • 1 cup strong dark ale or mead — Odin-approved for flavor and courage
  • 2 large onions, quartered — tears of the gods, to bring out the flavor
  • Fresh herbs for garnish — if you want to impress Freyja or just yourself

How to make it

  1. Preheat your hearth (oven) to 350°F (175°C). If you were a Viking, you’d have a roaring fire and maybe a few dwarves stoking it for you.
  2. Rub the venison leg with salt, pepper, and the crushed garlic. You want it seasoned like the sagas — strong and unforgettable.
  3. Mix the crushed juniper berries, thyme, and rosemary. This magical herb blend is Odin’s secret weapon against bland food (and maybe Loki’s mischief).
  4. Coat the meat with olive oil or melted butter, then rub the herb mix all over. Imagine the Valkyries blessing this meat before it enters your belly.
  5. Place the onions in the bottom of your roasting pan, and lay the venison on top. Onions are like the Asgardian shield — protecting the meat’s juicy goodness.
  6. Pour the dark ale or mead over the venison. Because every good Viking roast needs a little liquid courage.
  7. Roast in the oven for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare. Like Odin’s patience waiting for Ragnarok, this is a test of timing. No rushing!
  8. Let the meat rest for 15 minutes before slicing. Even Odin knows the value of patience — and letting the juices settle.
